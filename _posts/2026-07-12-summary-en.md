## [GPT-5.6 solves 50-year-old graph theory conjecture in under 1 hour](https://www.qbitai.com/2026/07/447873.html) ⭐️ 10.0/10

OpenAI's GPT-5.6 Sol Ultra model proved the Cycle Double Cover conjecture, an open problem in graph theory for nearly 50 years, in under an hour using 64 parallel sub-agents and a specially crafted prompt. This marks a paradigm shift in AI's capability for mathematical research, as an AI autonomously solved a long-standing open problem that had eluded human mathematicians for decades. It demonstrates the power of multi-agent systems and prompt engineering in tackling complex reasoning tasks. The model converted the problem into edge labeling over finite fields and solving linear equations, requiring each edge to be assigned two labels such that edges with the same label form cycles. The prompt (about 700 characters) did not prescribe steps but specified acceptance criteria, definitions, boundary conditions, and failure cases, with dynamic sub-agent allocation and independent review.

telegram · zaihuapd · Jul 12, 03:49

**Background**: The Cycle Double Cover conjecture asks whether every bridgeless undirected graph has a collection of cycles that together include each edge exactly twice. A bridge is an edge whose removal disconnects the graph. The conjecture was independently posed by Szekeres (1973) and Seymour (1979) and is related to graph embeddings. On July 10, 2026, OpenAI released a preprint claiming the proof generated by GPT-5.6.

This token inefficiency increases costs for Claude Code users and raises concerns about the tool's caching and harness design, especially compared to more frugal alternatives like OpenCode. It also sparks debate about whether Anthropic has financial incentives to inflate token usage. The study added logging between the coding tools and Anthropic's endpoint to capture all requests and usage data. ## [Zer0Fit: MCP Server for Google's TabFM and TimesFM Models](https://www.reddit.com/r/MachineLearning/comments/1uue8cc/zer0fit_i_took_googles_new_tabfm_timesfm_ml/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

A graduate student released Zer0Fit, an open-source MCP server that wraps Google's TabFM and TimesFM foundation models for zero-shot classification, regression, and time-series forecasting. The server runs locally in a Docker container and requires at least 16GB of VRAM on NVIDIA GPUs. This integration lowers the barrier to using state-of-the-art foundation models for tabular data and time-series tasks, allowing developers to perform ML tasks without training or tuning. It connects Google's models to popular AI interfaces like Open WebUI, Claude Code, and Codex, making zero-shot ML accessible to a wider audience. The server dynamically loads and unloads models with a 5-minute TTL to manage VRAM usage. It currently supports CSV input with plans to add XLS, XLSX, JSON, and JSONL. Performance on classic datasets includes 94.7% accuracy on Iris classification and R² of 0.91 on California housing regression.

reddit · r/MachineLearning · /u/Porespellar · Jul 12, 12:32

**Background**: TabFM and TimesFM are zero-shot foundation models released by Google Research for tabular data and time-series forecasting. TabFM handles classification and regression directly from table prompts, while TimesFM is a decoder-only model pretrained on extensive real-world time-series data. The Model Context Protocol (MCP) standardizes communication between AI applications and external tools, enabling LLMs to invoke ML models as tools.

A new study suggests that the shingles vaccine may reduce the risk of dementia, but critics highlight potential confounding due to health-seeking behavior. If confirmed, this could offer a simple, widely available intervention to reduce dementia burden. However, the debate underscores the importance of rigorous causal inference in epidemiology. The UK study used a hard age cutoff for vaccine eligibility, comparing dementia diagnoses between those just above and below the cutoff over seven years. The observed effect may be partly explained by reduced hospital visits among vaccinated individuals.

hackernews · saikatsg · Jul 12, 15:23 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48881874)

**Background**: Health-seeking behavior bias occurs when individuals who choose vaccination also engage in other health-promoting behaviors, leading to spurious associations. In epidemiological studies, this selection bias can distort results, as those who get vaccinated may be systematically different from those who do not.
